Sauteed Leeks with Julienne of Vegetables

Serves 6

2 tablespoon olive oil 
2 tablespoons butter
6 medium leeks, white part only, root end trimmed but intact, halved lengthwise 
1 red bell pepper, seeded and thinly sliced
1 carrot, peeled and thinly sliced
1/2 fennel bulb, cored and sliced
2 cloves garlic, minced
2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
1/4 cup dry vermouth

1. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, add 1 tablespoon of the oil and 1 tablespoon of the butter. Add the leeks, cut side down, and cook 2 minutes until lightly browned, turn and cook 2 minutes longer. Turn once more and add 1/3 cup water and 1/4 cup vermouth; cook until evaporated, turning leeks at least once, 8-10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper, remove from skillet and keep warm. 

2. To the skillet, add remaining 1 tablespoon oil and 1 tablespoon butter. Add the garlic, pepper, carrot and fennel; cook until crisp tender, about 3 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. 

3. Place 2 leek halves on each of six plates. Top leeks with vegetable mixture.
